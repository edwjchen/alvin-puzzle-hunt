This riddle is about word length and meaning. When you add letters to a 3-letter word, you might get a word that means "fewer" or "less".

Clue: Think of a 3-letter word that, when you add two specific letters, becomes a word meaning "fewer".

FEW
+ ER
= FEWER

üîç The Solution

Try the word "FEW" - it has 3 letters. When you add "ER" (2 letters), you get "FEWER" which means "fewer" or "less".

So the answer to the riddle is: FEW
