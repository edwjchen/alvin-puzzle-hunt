üí° What is a Prime Number?

A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.

Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19 are prime numbers.

Non-prime examples: 4 (divisible by 2), 6 (divisible by 2 and 3), 8 (divisible by 2 and 4), 9 (divisible by 3).

üìù Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1:

Identify all prime numbers between 1 and 20: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19

Step 2:

Add them together: 2 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 11 + 13 + 17 + 19

Step 3:

Calculate: 2 + 3 = 5, 5 + 5 = 10, 10 + 7 = 17, 17 + 11 = 28, 28 + 13 = 41, 41 + 17 = 58, 58 + 19 = 77

Answer:

77
